# T1 — Shell

> A rigid cuboid shell worn around the torso that replaces the body's curved, flexible trunk with a flat-surfaced, angular volume, constraining spinal rotation and forcing the wearer to move as a single block.

**Body region:** torso
**Build time:** 20–25 minutes

---

## Parts required

Cut and lay out all parts before beginning assembly.

| Qty | Part | Material | Dimensions | Notes |
|-----|------|----------|------------|-------|
| 2 | Wide panel (front/back) | Double-wall cardboard | 50 × 40 cm | Front and back faces of the shell |
| 2 | Narrow panel (sides) | Double-wall cardboard | 50 × 30 cm | Left and right faces |
| 2 | Shoulder tube | Wide tube (Ø 4–6 cm) | 30 cm long | Span across the top, rest on shoulders, carry the shell's weight |
| 2 | Handle tube | Narrow tube (Ø 2–3 cm) | 20 cm long | Mounted inside for the wearer to grip and stabilise |
| — | Packing tape | — | ~3 m | |
| — | Masking tape | — | ~1.5 m | Edge protection, shoulder padding |

## Assembly

### Step 1

> Lay the two **wide panels** and two **narrow panels** flat in a row, alternating: wide — narrow — wide — narrow. Leave a 3 mm gap between each panel (the thickness of a pencil). Tape them together along the gaps using long strips of packing tape on one side. This creates a hinged, foldable strip.

### Step 2

> Stand the strip upright and fold it into a rectangular tube — wide panels at front and back, narrow panels at sides. Tape the final edge joint (where the first and last panels meet) with packing tape inside and outside.

*You now have an open-topped, open-bottomed rectangular box.*

### Step 3

> Take the two **shoulder tubes**. Position them across the inside of the box, spanning from the front panel to the back panel, roughly 5 cm below the top edge. Space them at shoulder width (~25 cm apart). Tape each end firmly to the inside face of the front and back panels.

### Step 4

> Wrap masking tape thickly around the centre section of each shoulder tube where it will rest on the wearer's shoulders — this is the primary comfort padding.

### Step 5

> Take the two **handle tubes**. Tape one to the inside of each side panel, vertically, at a comfortable gripping height (roughly midway). These let the wearer stabilise the shell with their hands.

### Step 6

> Tape masking tape along all interior top and bottom edges. Round any corners that could catch clothing.

---

## Fit and adjust

Lift the shell over the head and lower it so the shoulder tubes rest on the shoulders. The bottom edge should fall between the hip and mid-thigh. Grip the handle tubes to stabilise.

**Check:** Breathing — the shell is open top and bottom, allowing free airflow. Arm openings — the wearer can extend both arms through the open top. Removal — the wearer can lift the shell off overhead without assistance.

**Adjust:** If the shell is too tight around the torso, cut the tape on one side joint, add a strip of cardboard to widen, and re-tape. If it drops too low, shorten the panels from the bottom.

---

## Movement notes

The shell eliminates independent shoulder-hip rotation. Turning the shoulders now turns the entire torso as a unit. Bending forward is blocked by the rigid front panel — the wearer must hinge at the hips. Reaching sideways is constrained by the narrow panels. The wearer discovers how much everyday movement depends on spinal flexibility, and begins to find new ways to express intention using only whole-body rotation, tilt, and weight shift — exactly the vocabulary available to a rigid-bodied robot.

---

## Design bridge

This is the most direct body-to-robot analogy in the catalog. The shell gives the wearer first-hand experience of being a cuboid. The movement constraints — no spinal twist, no lateral bend, no independent shoulder action — mirror the constraints of the two-cuboid robot's lower body exactly. Movement qualities discovered here (heavy rotation, committed direction changes, the expressiveness of tilt) translate directly to servos C (pan) and D (base tilt). Use `serial_control.ino` to explore: try `C60` to `C120` sweeps at different `S` values to match the rotation speed you experienced in the shell.

---

## Variations

- **Half shell:** Remove the back panel for an open-backed version that constrains the front but allows more spinal extension backward.
- **Tall and narrow:** Increase height to 60 cm and reduce width to 25 cm — creates a column-like torso that constrains differently.
- **Segmented:** Cut the shell in half horizontally and reconnect with tape hinges to create a two-segment torso that can tilt at the waist — a direct analogue of the two-cuboid robot's hinge joint.
