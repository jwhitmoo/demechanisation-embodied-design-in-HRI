# Performance-Based Exercises

← [Main README](../README.md) · [Workshop methodology](../methodology/README.md) · [Wearables catalog](../wearables/README.md) · [Robotics](../robotics/README.md)

This section catalogues the movement and performance exercises used in the toolkit, organised into three progressive categories. The exercises draw on Augusto Boal's de-mechanisation techniques from *Theatre of the Oppressed*, adapted for the context of embodied robot design.

## Categories

### [`warm-ups/`](warm-ups/)

Low-stakes preparatory exercises that build body awareness and group trust before the main de-mechanisation work.

- [Slow-Motion Weight Transfer](warm-ups/slow-motion-weight-transfer.md)

### [`de-mechanisation/`](de-mechanisation/)

Core exercises derived from Boal's practice. These disrupt habitual bodily patterns — the "mechanised" routines we perform without awareness — and open space for new, imaginative movement vocabularies. Exercises are designed to be performed both with and without the wearable body extensions from Phase 1.

Key exercises still to be documented:

- **Gravity negotiation** — Explore how your body relates to gravity when familiar balance points are altered by a wearable extension.
- **Rhythm and pause** — Move through space with an externally imposed rhythm, then freeze; discover the expressive potential of stillness and timing.

### [`movement-exploration/`](movement-exploration/)

Exercises that specifically target the design of non-anthropomorphic robotic movement. Participants use their bodies (optionally extended by wearables) to explore movement qualities such as weight, flow, time, and space — drawing on Rudolf Laban's Effort framework — and then describe those qualities in terms that can inform servo parameters.

- [Effort Qualities for Robotic Movement](movement-exploration/effort-qualities-mapping.md)

## Exercise Card Format

Each exercise is documented as a Markdown file following this structure:

```
# Exercise Name

**Category:** warm-up | de-mechanisation | movement-exploration
**Duration:** approximate time
**Group size:** individual | pairs | small group | full group
**Wearables required:** none | specific extension(s)

## Objective

What participants should experience or discover.

## Setup

Space, materials, and facilitator preparation.

## Instructions

Step-by-step facilitation script.

## Reflection Prompts

Questions to ask after the exercise.

## Design Bridge

How insights from this exercise connect to robotic prototyping decisions.
```
